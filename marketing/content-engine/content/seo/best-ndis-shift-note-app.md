If you're a support worker writing progress notes from memory at the end of a
long day, the right app gives you your evenings back. Here's an honest rundown of
the options in 2026 — including where each one fits.

## What to look for in a shift note app

- **Fast capture in the moment** — ideally voice or a few taps, one-handed, on a phone.
- **Structure that holds up** — prompts for the bones of a good note (when, who, goal, what happened, response).
- **Person-first, factual** — supports respectful, observation-based language.
- **Works for how you work** — sole trader vs team; offline on a phone.
- **Privacy** — participant notes are sensitive information under the Privacy Act 1988; check how data is handled and stored.
- **Fair pricing** — per-worker pricing matters if you're a sole trader.

### Clio (Clio Care)
Targets individual workers with voice-driven NDIS note generation. Often a free
period then a low monthly fee. A good fit if you want voice-first notes as a
standalone.

### ShiftCare
A broader provider/team platform — rostering, billing and notes — with
speech-to-text inside it. Best for organisations managing teams, rather than a
solo worker who just wants fast notes.

### Lumary / SupportAbility / Brevity / CareMaster
Established provider case-management systems built for organisations (rostering +
billing + compliance + notes), typically per-user or enterprise pricing. Powerful
for medium/large providers; heavier than a sole trader needs for notes alone.

### The honest "free" option: your phone's voice memo or notes app
Capturing a voice memo or three bullet points in the moment, then tidying later,
genuinely beats a blank page at home. It just won't structure the note or keep
your records organised for you — which is the gap purpose-built tools fill.

## How to choose

- **Sole trader who just wants fast, structured notes:** a worker-first tool like Caira or Clio.
- **Small/medium provider managing a team:** ShiftCare or a case-management system.
- **Just starting / on a budget:** begin with capture-in-the-moment on your phone, then upgrade when the admin starts hurting.

## FAQ

**What's the best NDIS shift note app for a sole trader?**
A phone-first, worker-priced tool that captures notes in the moment and structures
them for you — Caira and Clio are built for this; ShiftCare and the larger
case-management systems are aimed at teams.

**Do I have to write notes at the end of the day?**
No — the biggest time-saver is capturing in the moment (voice or a few taps) and
tidying later, while the detail's fresh.

**Are these apps compliant with NDIS requirements?**
A tool can *help* you write clear, factual, goal-linked notes, but compliance
depends on your practice and your provider's policies — no app can guarantee it.
Always follow current NDIS rules and your provider's procedures.

**How should participant data be handled?**
Participant notes are sensitive information under the Privacy Act 1988. Check how
any tool stores and protects data before you use it for real notes.
