The Audit-Ready Shift Note Checklist

A free one-pager from Edward at Caira — a support worker who got sick of writing notes from memory at 9pm.

This is general guidance to help you write your own clear, factual notes. It is not legal or compliance advice, and it does not guarantee any audit outcome. Always follow your provider's policies and the current NDIS rules.

The 5 bones of a note that holds up

  • When — date and start/finish time of the support
  • Who — participant and who else was present
  • Goal — which support goal this shift was working toward
  • What happened — what you actually did and what the participant did
  • Response — how the participant responded / any change observed

The factual-language test

  • Written in plain language a stranger could follow in two years
  • States observations, not opinions dressed up as facts ("declined lunch" ✓ — "was being difficult" ✗)
  • Person-first and respectful (about the person, not a label)
  • Any incident recorded calmly: what / when / who / what you did / who you told
  • No guesses at cause written as if confirmed

The habit that makes it painless

  • Captured in the moment, not reconstructed at home (voice memo, notes app, or paper — then tidy later)
  • Written, checked and saved same day
  • Stored consistently — one place per participant, same structure each time
Quick self-check: If someone read this note in two years with zero context, would they understand what happened, why, and how the participant responded? If yes — you're done. If no — add the missing bone above.
