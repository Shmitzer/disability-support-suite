# Caira Launch Playbook: A Ground-Up, Founder-Led, Organic-First Strategy

> This is the canonical strategy the content engine is built to serve.
> The `config.json`, `content/arc.json` and compliance checks all derive from it.

## TL;DR
- **Win the warm network first, then let Facebook and founder-led content compound.** 6+ years inside the NDIS sector on the Central Coast is the unfair advantage. Warm, founder-led launches convert far better than cold outreach (~25–40% reply, 50–65% first-meeting-to-opportunity). Sell to colleagues and small providers you know, turn 5–10 into named case studies, then expand. Facebook groups (where this ~70%-female, casual, mobile-first workforce lives) and personal LinkedIn/founder content are the two channels that matter.
- **AI is a force-multiplier for content and discoverability, not an autopilot.** Great for "one idea → many posts" and a GEO/SEO base so Caira gets recommended by ChatGPT. Autonomous post/comment/DM agents are a real account-ban risk; AI-referral traffic is still ~1% of web traffic. AI helps you produce and get found — not a primary traffic firehose yet.
- **Be patient: meaningful organic + AI traffic takes 6–12 months to compound.** First paying customers from the warm network in month 1–2; first community/content signups ~month 3; a real organic engine by months 6–12. Track activation (notes logged per worker per week), not vanity metrics.

## Channel priority for Caira
**Facebook (communities) → founder LinkedIn → Instagram → TikTok.**

- **Facebook** — #1 for both frontline workers and small providers. Education-first; be a helpful member first, vendor second. Own a Page (+ eventually a Central Coast worker group — an identified gap).
- **LinkedIn** — #1 for the buyer tier (provider owners, coordinators, plan managers). Post from your **personal profile** (5–7x reach), 3–5x/week, links in first comment. Build-in-public home.
- **Instagram** — secondary; repurposed reels endpoint.
- **TikTok** — opportunistic; a one-handed voice-note demo is ideal short-form, but ~30-day ramp, leads ~day 60–90. Experiment, don't build on it.

## Community-led growth (the heart of it)
Communities convert at 30–50% when trusted, 0% when not. Use your real name; be a support worker who built a tool. Earn the right to mention Caira (~50 genuinely helpful comments before pitching). Target groups: NDIS Grassroots Discussion (~54K), NDIS Community (~15K), NDIS Service Providers Discussion (~13K), Support Coordinators/Plan Managers groups, regional NSW groups, r/NDIS.

## AI usage — the honest picture
- **DO:** AI-assisted content creation & repurposing (ChatGPT + Canva), lightweight scheduling (Metricool/Buffer free tiers), and GEO (structured Q&A/comparison/how-to pages so AI engines cite Caira). Keep a human edit on everything.
- **DON'T:** autonomous agents that post/comment/DM. Platform-ban risk (LinkedIn restricted ~23% of automation users within 90 days in a 2026 test; banned Apollo.io & Seamless.ai in 2025). No astroturfing, fake reviews, or sockpuppets — detectable and fatal in a small trust-based sector.

## Conversion & funnel
- Lean toward a **free trial / reverse trial**, not pure freemium (freemium ~2–8%; opt-in trial ~8–22%; opt-out card trial ~35–55%). Caira is fast-to-value (log a note in 90s) — keep signup near-frictionless. Benchmark Clio: free then ~$12/mo.
- **Landing page:** <2s load, 30s one-handed voice-note demo above the fold, single CTA ("Start logging notes free"), founding-user social proof, WCAG 2.1.
- **Lead magnets:** NDIS-compliant shift-note templates, person-first language cheat-sheet, "audit-ready note" checklist, plain-English compliance guide.
- **Email:** 4–5 email nurture (deliver magnet → value → founder story → case study → soft trial CTA).

## SEO / GEO foundation
Comparison pages ("Caira vs spreadsheets/paper", honest "alternatives"), bottom-funnel "best NDIS shift note app", NDIS-documentation how-tos. Local Central Coast/NSW angle is low-competition. Slow (6–12 mo) but the cheapest durable channel; the substrate GEO needs.

## Timeline & metrics
- **Phase 0 (wk 0–4):** positioning ("the shift logger built by a support worker"), landing page + 1 lead magnet, AI stack, claim profiles, 8–12 seed posts, join groups & be helpful, line up 5–10 warm beta users.
- **Phase 1 (mo 1–2):** onboard warm beta; first paying customers from people you know; 3–5 posts/wk; collect testimonials. Metric: **activation** (notes/worker/week), beta→paid.
- **Phase 2 (mo 3–4):** 3–5 case studies; sparing community mentions; referral loop; comparison/how-to content. Metric: signups by source, trial→paid, referrals/customer.
- **Phase 3 (mo 5–6):** host own community; AI repurposing engine; TikTok experiment. Metric: organic signups/wk, MRR, churn, CAC by channel.
- **Phase 4 (mo 6–12):** organic + content + GEO compounding. Metric: MRR growth, payback, organic vs warm share.

## Australia / NDIS compliance (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
- **Never** use "NDIS approved/endorsed" or imply NDIA affiliation. Never overstate outcomes. Substantiate every claim. ACCC/NDIA/NDIS Commission actively penalise misleading NDIS advertising (Vorwerk/Thermomix $79,200 and Bedshed $39,600 in 2025; WeFlex $19,800 June 2026).
- Position Caira as a tool that **helps workers write their own compliant notes** — never as something that *guarantees* compliance.
- Participant notes are **sensitive information under the Privacy Act 1988** — lead on privacy & data handling as a trust differentiator.
- **Watch:** April 2026 NDIS reforms (growth cap, crackdown on unregistered providers) may reshape the sole-trader segment — be ready to pivot toward small registered providers/coordinators.

## Decision thresholds
- Warm beta→paid **<20%** → fix product/onboarding/pricing before scaling channels.
- 3 consecutive months of organic signup growth + healthy activation → invest more in content/TikTok/GEO.
- Channel CAC **> ~$50** → deprioritise (community/referral near-zero; content $20–40).
- Reconsider freemium-vs-trial only with enough volume to A/B an opt-out trial.

## Caveats
Much AI-traffic/GEO/build-in-public content is vendor-published (promotional bias); directional facts are corroborated, specific tool rankings/stats are indicative. Facebook group counts may be stale — verify live. Conversion benchmarks are bimodal — set direction, not precise targets. Competitive landscape (Clio, "AI shift notes") moves fast — reassess quarterly. April 2026 reforms are a live risk. Assumes part-time/solo build → timelines 2–3x; guard against burnout.
