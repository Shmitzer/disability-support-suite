# Caira — Founder Transition Plan (casual DSW → full-time founder)

*Companion to `STRATEGIC_REVIEW_V2.md`. Personal/career transition model for moving from a casual
Disability Support Worker role to running Caira full-time.*

> Planning model, **not financial/legal/tax advice.** AU structure, super, and income-protection
> specifics need a qualified accountant. Figures are placeholders against real, stated inputs.

## TLDR
Don't quit — **fade out one shift at a time, each turn paid for by a signed pilot, a closed round,
or replaced income.** The casual DSW role is your bridge, your customer-discovery channel, and your
founder story — exploit it until Caira can pay you to leave. **Personal monthly burn: $8k. Target
personal cash cushion before going full-time: ~$72k (9 months). Real "quit" trigger: seed round
closes with a $10k/mo founder salary** (covers the $8k burn with a $2k buffer for super/income
protection/savings).

## Core numbers

| Input | Value |
|---|---|
| Personal monthly burn | **$8,000** |
| Target founder salary (full-time) | **$10,000/mo** ($2k buffer over burn) |
| Personal cash cushion before leap | **~$72k (9 months)** — min $48k / safe $96k |
| Casual DSW income (bridge) | ~$250–400/shift → 2 shifts/wk ≈ $2.5–3.5k/mo (~30–40% of burn) |

## Runway cushion targets (personal cash, separate from company)

| Buffer | Cash needed |
|---|---|
| Minimum (6 mo) | $48k |
| Comfortable (9 mo) — **target** | $72k |
| Safe (12 mo) | $96k |

## Fade-out triggers (turn the dial, don't leap)

| Stage | Trigger | Income replacement |
|---|---|---|
| Drop 1 shift/wk | First 2 paid pilots signed | ~$1.5–2k/mo (pilot rev or savings draw) |
| Drop to 1–2 shifts/wk | Caira MRR + partial founder pay ≈ $4k/mo | ~half of burn |
| **Go full-time** | **Seed closes with $10k/mo founder salary**, OR Caira covers $8k/mo for 3 straight months | Full ($10k covers $8k + $2k buffer) |

**Rule: never give up income faster than Caira or investor money replaces it.** Pilot MRR alone is
unlikely to hit $8k/mo (~$96k ARR) before you'd want to go full-time — so the realistic full-time
trigger is the **seed closing with a founder salary**, not MRR covering your life. Pilots exist to
*unlock the raise*; the raise pays the $10k.

## Founder-salary impact on the raise
- 2 founders × $10k = **$20k/mo = $240k/yr**.
- On a ~$1M seed that's ~36% of the raise — within norms, but nudges toward raising at the **upper
  end (~$1.1–1.2M)** so salary doesn't eat build/hiring budget.
- Prefer **raising ~$150–200k more** over shortening runway to fund salary.
- Keep comp "survivable, not optimized-for" — $10k reads right; past ~$12–13k pre-traction looks like
  salary over growth. Watch Rule of 40 / runway.

## Exploit the DSW role before leaving
- **Customer discovery** — you are the user; every shift is product research. Document pains.
- **Design partners / pilots** — your provider and peers are warm leads; convert them while inside.
- **Founder credibility** — "built by a frontline DSW who lived the compliance pain" is domain
  authority investors pay a premium for. It's the strongest part of the seed narrative.

## Boring-but-critical AU structure (with an accountant)
- **Incorporate** before pilot revenue or investor money (caps personal liability — matters given the
  safeguarding profile).
- **R&D Tax Incentive** — rebates a large share of dev spend; non-dilutive runway. Register early.
- **Super + income protection** — thin as a casual, zero as a founder unless arranged. The $2k/mo
  salary buffer is partly for this.
- **Clean personal/company money separation** from day one.

## Protect the human
Keep 1–2 shifts longer than strictly necessary — for income, structure, user connection, and a
psychological safety net. Founders who aren't desperate make better decisions. Burnout torches more
startups than bad markets.

## The two numbers to hold
1. **$72k** — personal cash cushion before the leap.
2. **$10k/mo founder salary** — the raise line item that replaces your income. The round closing is
   the real "quit" trigger.
