# Caira — Co-founder Search (commercial/clinical, NDIS sector authority)

*Companion to `NEXT_MOVES.md` (#5) and `FOUNDER_TRANSITION.md`. How to find, vet, and structure a
domain-credible co-founder. Finding a co-founder is recruiting + dating, not a job posting.*

## TLDR
Run the co-founder search and the **paid-pilot search as the same motion** — the provider managers
you pitch for pilots are your co-founder pool, and a co-founder helps you close pilots. Start with
your own DSW network. Target **sector credibility + a buyer network + willingness to sell.** Date
before you marry (3-month trial project), commit slowly, paper it with vesting. **Don't take the
first warm body — a wrong co-founder is worse than none.**

## Target profile
Non-negotiables: **sector credibility · a buyer network · willingness to sell.** Nice-to-have: prior
startup exposure (don't over-index — sector authority is harder to acquire than startup instinct).

- **Ex-provider operator** — ran ops, quality/compliance, or service delivery at an NDIS provider;
  lived the pain, has a buyer contact book.
- **Allied health / clinical lead** — senior OT, physio, behaviour support, or support coordinator,
  credible but frustrated by admin/compliance burden.
- **Quality & safeguarding background** — done NDIS audits/registration. Rare and gold: de-risks the
  single biggest liability.

## Where to find them (AU/NDIS)
1. **Your own DSW network — start here.** Best source. List every senior coordinator, team lead,
   allied health, or manager you've worked with or who impressed you; start conversations.
2. **LinkedIn, searched deliberately.** Titles: Service Manager, Quality & Compliance Manager,
   Operations Manager, Practice Lead, Support Coordination Manager at NDIS providers. Prioritise
   people *posting* about sector frustrations — pre-validated on the pain.
3. **Sector communities & events** — provider conferences, NDIS/disability meetups, allied health
   association events, LinkedIn groups, local disability forums.
4. **Pilot conversations.** The provider managers you pitch are exactly this profile; a champion who
   keeps leaning in is a co-founder candidate.
5. **Warm intros from advisors.** Recruit 1–2 NDIS-sector advisors first (lower commitment), ask each
   "who's the best restless operator you know?"
6. **Startup co-founder channels (backstop)** — co-founder matching platforms, AU accelerator /
   healthtech-impact tracks, university health/social-work entrepreneurship programs.

## How to approach
- **Lead with the problem, not the proposal:** "I'm building something for the compliance/reporting
  pain you deal with — can I show you and get your honest take?" Validates the product and auditions
  them at once.
- **Date before you marry:** work together on something real first — a pilot, a sales call, a
  compliance doc. A **3-month paid-or-equity trial project** reveals more than any coffee.
- **Look for pull, not push:** the right person starts doing the work before there's a deal. If you
  have to convince them, they're wrong.

## Evaluate hard
1. **Do they bring buyers?** Can they open 5 provider doors next month? That's the point.
2. **Will they actually sell?** Clinical people sometimes hate selling — you need someone who picks up
   the phone.
3. **Safeguarding values alignment** — they must take vulnerable-population duty of care as seriously
   as you do. Non-negotiable given the risk profile.
4. **Resilience + commitment** — willing to reduce hours / forgo early salary the way you are.
   Asymmetric commitment kills startups.

## Structuring it
- **Meaningful equity** — real co-founder, not employee: commonly **15–40%** depending on
  timing/contribution (later = less). Cheap equity now buys a fundable company.
- **4-year vesting, 1-year cliff on BOTH stakes** — protects you both if someone walks; investors
  require it anyway.
- **Written founder agreement** before money/emotions are involved — roles, equity,
  decision-making, leaver terms.
- **AU startup lawyer** to paper it.

## Sequencing & caution
Run co-founder + pilot search as one motion; DSW shifts are the discovery channel for both. **Meet
candidates quickly, commit slowly via a trial project. Don't take the first warm body** — a wrong
co-founder is hard to remove, owns a chunk, and can sink the raise. Better a few months solo with
advisors than a bad marriage.
